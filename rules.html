Rules & Regulations

Important guidelines and policies for all students

Dress Code

General Guidelines

  • Students must wear clean, neat, and appropriate clothing
  • No revealing or offensive clothing
  • Footwear must be worn at all times
  • University ID must be visible

Specific Requirements

  • Lab sessions: Closed-toe shoes and lab coats
  • Sports activities: Appropriate athletic wear
  • Formal events: Business casual attire

Attendance Policy

Minimum Attendance Requirements

  • 75% attendance is mandatory for all courses
  • Attendance is tracked through the university portal
  • Medical leave requires proper documentation

Consequences

  • Warning after 3 consecutive absences
  • Course failure if attendance falls below 75%
  • Special consideration for documented medical conditions

Academic Integrity

Plagiarism Policy

  • All work must be original and properly cited
  • Use of plagiarism detection software
  • Zero tolerance for academic dishonesty

Penalties

  • First offense: Warning and grade reduction
  • Second offense: Course failure
  • Third offense: Academic probation

Campus Conduct

General Behavior

  • Respect for all members of the university community
  • No harassment or discrimination
  • Maintain campus cleanliness

Disciplinary Actions

  • Verbal warning for minor violations
  • Written warning for repeated violations
  • Disciplinary committee review for serious violations

Library Rules

General Guidelines

  • Maintain silence in designated quiet areas
  • No food or drinks in the library
  • Return books on time
  • Use library resources responsibly

Borrowing Rules

  • Maximum 3 books at a time
  • 2-week borrowing period
  • Renewal allowed if no holds
  • Late fees apply for overdue items

Batch Bond

General Guidelines

  • There must be REP or REPI in the batch (proceeded via voting and evaluation of student contributions)
  • All juniors must complete tasks assigned by seniors (seniors may ask about university rules and details)
  • All students must update their university journey, skills, and contributions in their profile section
  • All students must create an account in CampusBook (CBOOK)

Rules

  • Seniors must check juniors' academics
  • Juniors should report unusual activities among batchmates to seniors
  • If juniors have problems, inform seniors first; they may escalate to the Top Board together
  • Juniors must respect seniors and batchmates

Random Card Game

Game Overview

In this game all students will receive random cool nicknames. The event will be held in the auditorium after completing the tasks mentioned in the Rules & Regulations section. This nickname (card) will be used throughout the university journey and will be known among batchmates.
